STEP 1 · BUILD YOUR LETTER

Build your SB 1090 support letter

The Advocates Portal and the committee consultants want a signed letter, not pasted text. Download the PDF, print and sign it (or add a digital signature), and upload or attach it. Want to add letterhead first? Download the Word version, edit and sign, then save as PDF.

STEP 2 · SEND YOUR LETTER

Send your letter to the committee

Deadline: Wednesday, June 24, 2026 · 5:00 p.m. for the July 1 hearing. A signed letter in the committee's official file is the single highest-impact thing you can do.

Submit your letter — two ways. The Portal is the committee's official record; the one-click email is the easiest. Either one works — doing both is great.

OPTION A · OFFICIAL

Submit on the Advocates Portal

  1. Open the portal and create a free account.
  2. Search SB 1090 and select it.
  3. Set your position to Support.
  4. File to BOTH committees — submit once to Assembly Housing & Community Development and again to Assembly Local Government. SB 1090 is heard by both on July 1, and a letter only counts in the committee it's filed with. Same letter both times.
  5. Upload your letter and submit (then repeat for the second committee).
Open the Advocates Portal →
OPTION B · EASIEST

Send it in one click

Opens one pre-filled support email addressed to the staff of both committees (Housing & Community Development and Local Government) in Gmail or your mail app — just add your name and press send (attach your downloaded letter if you like). One click covers both. A copy comes to our team, so we'll also file it to the Portal for you.

Every adult in your household should file their own letter — that's how we reach 500+. Please cc Sen. Pérez (author) and Asm. Harabedian (co-author) if you can.

STEP 3 · CALL THE GOVERNOR

Ask Gov. Newsom for an Executive Order

The fastest protection of all. The Governor extended this exact relief to the Pacific Palisades within days of the fire (Executive Order N-32-25); he can do the same for the entire Eaton Fire area with a stroke of the pen — and unlike SB 1090, an order can reach back to the speculative subdivisions already filed since the fire. Two minutes:

📞 Call the Governor: (916) 445-2841 ✉ Message the Governor's office →
What to say (30 seconds):
  • “I'm calling to ask Governor Newsom to protect Altadena's recovery from the Eaton Fire.”
  • Please grant an executive order protecting the Altadena / Eaton Fire disaster recovery area.
  • It would pause the SB 1123 by-right lot-splitting that lets out-of-town developers carve burned lots into up to ten units before families can even return.
  • A community still grieving and rebuilding shouldn't be the easiest place in the state to subdivide.
  • “Please act now. Thank you.”
