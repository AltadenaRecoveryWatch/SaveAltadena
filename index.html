What's happening

After the Eaton Fire, state law SB 1123 lets speculators subdivide a burned single-family lot into up to 10 units — by right, with no public hearing. A companion bill, SB 1116 — which would strengthen the law and even void private deed restrictions (CC&Rs) that limit this density (except in HOA communities) — has passed the State Senate. The bill meant to protect us — SB 1090 — is not law yet. So right now, Altadena has no protection in place.

This isn't hypothetical. At least ten subdivisions are already confirmed in Los Angeles County records, together carving ten burned lots into more than eighty — with developers racing to lock in their rights. Investor purchases jumped from about 5% of Altadena home sales before the fire to nearly 50% after.

After the same fire, the Palisades got an emergency order suspending SB 9 — but it was keyed to the fire-hazard-severity-zone map. The Palisades sits in those zones; most of Altadena's burned neighborhoods don't — so the very same order left most of Altadena out. Same fire, same emergency — different protection.

We didn't get a protection written for us — we got a hand-me-down cut to fit the Palisades. But the Governor's emergency powers cover the entire declared disaster area, which includes all of Altadena — not a hazard map. He can protect the disaster area, not the map, and cover both SB 9 and SB 1123. The power exists right now; what's missing is the will to use it.

We support residents rebuilding, ADUs, and density neighbors choose. We oppose outside speculators displacing fire survivors. This is about a fair recovery — not blocking housing.

What we're asking for

1

Emergency executive order — now

The Governor can suspend these density laws in Altadena under his emergency powers. The Palisades got that protection — but tied to a fire-hazard map that leaves most of Altadena out. We're asking him to protect the whole disaster area, not the hazard map (covering both SB 9 and SB 1123), and asking Supervisor Barger, Assemblymember Harabedian, and Senator Pérez to all press him to act immediately.

2

Pass and strengthen SB 1090

Make its protection reach back to the fire date (Jan 7, 2025) and cover any project without an issued building permit — so speculators can't race to lock in deals first.

3

A community master plan — and accountability

Plan the recovery with residents, and make sure rising land values benefit survivors, not speculators.
