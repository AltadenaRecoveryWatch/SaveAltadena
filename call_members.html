📞 Your 30-second call script

“Hi, my name is [your name], and I'm a constituent calling from [your city]. I'm asking the Assemblymember to vote AYE on SB 1090 — and, just as urgently, to make it take effect immediately. This can't wait: every day of delay, speculators file new subdivisions and lock up more burned Altadena lots before survivors can rebuild.

SB 1090 puts a narrow, temporary pause on urban lot splits in Altadena, protecting responsible and resilient recovery pathways while taking no housing away. We deserve parity and should have the same protection as the Palisades. Second, it should not be easier for a developer to build than for a survivor to rebuild.

And we support the amendment that allows SB 9 projects for 100% deed-restricted low-to-moderate-income affordable housing — so Altadena can rebuild the income diversity that makes it special and bring renters and working families home.

Please vote AYE on SB 1090 — and make it effective as soon as possible. Every day of delay hurts Altadena's fire survivors. Thank you.”

Reach voicemail? Leave the same message — name, city, and “please vote AYE on SB 1090, and make it effective as soon as possible — this is urgent for Altadena's fire survivors.” It still counts.
