Rank your top 3 priorities for SB 1090

Sacramento is weighing whether — and how — to carve out SB 9 from the SB 1090 moratorium. Tell us what matters most so we represent Altadena accurately.

The community has asked for two things that are in tension with each other: a moratorium covering both SB 1123 and SB 9 projects, and specific carve-outs within SB 9 for owner-occupants, fire survivors, and community land trusts / affordable-housing developers. The more carve-outs we ask for, the harder it becomes to defend our core argument — that Altadena deserves the same treatment as the Palisades. Opponents (YIMBY, the California Apartment Association) are pushing amendments that would weaken the moratorium, and Assemblymember Wicks has made clear we need to address the SB 9 question directly — that was the heart of the July 1 hearing.

What we know
  • SB 1090 and the moratorium on SB 1123 projects are essential — passing it with urgency needs a two-thirds vote.
  • The bill as written doesn't yet include every amendment Altadena has asked for, and we're not certain we can win them all.
  • Excluding SB 9 entirely from the moratorium would resolve that fight — but it would also leave the door open for developers to profit and build poorly designed projects here.
  • At the same time, opposition is pushing amendment language that would burst the door wide open on SB 9 — and create backdoors for SB 1123-type projects.
