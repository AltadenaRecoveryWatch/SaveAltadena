What is disaster capitalism?

When investors exploit a disaster for profit. In Altadena, one developer shows exactly how — seven burned lots, a separate single-purpose LP for each.

Woody Gruninger (Stone West Homes / Stonewall Construction) is among the most active out-of-town developers we've identified — tied through public records to a string of fire-destroyed Altadena lots being subdivided under SB 1123, each held by its own single-purpose LP. He also wrote the opposition letter asking Sacramento to kill SB 1090, the bill that would pause exactly what he's doing. Here's the record.

The Pattern — Systematic Disaster Capitalism

The public record shows a coordinated, fast-moving operation:

  • A separate single-purpose LP for each lot — formed starting October 2025, months after the fire.
  • Subdivision maps filed in rapid succession (two completed the same day, April 10, 2026).
  • $83,000–$175,000 paid per future lot — a fraction of the ~$585,000 a single Altadena lot sells for. This is disaster capitalism.
  • A burst of acquisitions while neighbors are still displaced — and a personal stake in keeping the loophole open.
Then he asked Sacramento to protect it: the same developer assembling seven burned lots into ~60 by-right units wrote the opposition letter urging the Legislature to defeat SB 1090 — the one bill that would pause this exact pathway while survivors come home.

How do I know if Woody is buying a lot? Because Peter Miller is his agent.

Public MLS records show Miller represented the buyer's side on every recorded Gruninger acquisition — each lot then placed into its own single-purpose LP and raced toward subdivision.

By contrast, the burned lots not tied to Mr. Gruninger were bought through different agents entirely. That makes Mr. Miller the clearest fingerprint of this operation: the same agent, sourcing burned family lot after burned family lot for the same developer. These are not a series of unrelated sales — they are one assembled pipeline.
