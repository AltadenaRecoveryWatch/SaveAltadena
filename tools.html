📞 Phone-call packet

The 30-second script plus the full call list — all 22 committee members with phone numbers and a tally column. Print it and work the list.

Your 30-second call script

“Hi, my name is [your name], and I'm a constituent calling from [your city]. I'm asking the Assemblymember to vote AYE on SB 1090 — and to pass it as written, with no amendments.”

After the Eaton Fire, out-of-town developers are using SB 1123 to split burned Altadena lots into as many as ten units — by right, with no hearing — before families can even return. SB 1090 puts a narrow, temporary pause on that, and it takes no housing away.

Two quick reasons: First, parity — the Palisades already got this exact protection by executive order after the same fire; Altadena lost even more and deserves the same. Second, fairness — a survivor can rebuild only about 10% larger, while a developer next door gets ten times the units. That's disaster capitalism, not recovery.

“Please vote AYE on SB 1090 and pass it as written. Thank you.”
