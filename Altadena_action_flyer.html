Eaton Fire Recovery · Community Action

Stop speculators from taking Altadena

A fair recovery — not a fire sale.
As of June 12, 2026 · verify bill status before circulating

What's happening

After the Eaton Fire, outside investors are buying burned lots from survivors — often elderly, underinsured families — for far less than they're worth, then using a state law, SB 1123, to pack up to 10 units onto a single family's lot with no public hearing. Many sellers may not be getting fair value for their land. A pending bill (SB 1116) would make this harder to stop; another (SB 1090) is meant to protect us — but as written it starts too late and lets too many projects through.

Altadena has long been a diverse, family-rooted community. Unchecked speculation strips generational wealth and displaces people who've lived here for decades. This isn't about blocking housing — it's about protecting survivors.

It's also a safety problem. Altadena's narrow, often single-access foothill streets just failed in a deadly evacuation. Packing up to 10 units onto each burned lot — with no sidewalks, septic systems instead of public sewer, and undersized roads — overwhelms the evacuation routes and infrastructure these blocks were never built for. It also shows these lots don't fit the urban-infill premise SB 1123 assumes.

The three things we're asking for

1

Demand emergency action — Supervisor Barger must press the Governor for an executive order

The Palisades got an emergency order — but tied to a fire-hazard map that leaves most of Altadena out. We didn't get a protection written for us; we got a hand-me-down that doesn't fit. As our representative and SB 1090's sponsor, Supervisor Barger has the standing to fix it — she must press the Governor for an order protecting the whole Altadena disaster area now (covering the SB 1123 lot-split pathway). SB 1090 doesn't take effect until 2027, and survivors can't wait.

2

Pass and strengthen SB 1090

Make the protection apply from the fire date, January 7, 2025, through January 7, 2030, to every project without an issued building permit — so speculators can't race to file before it takes effect. SB 1090 is still pending and may only protect going forward, which is why the emergency order can't wait.

3

Carve Altadena out of SB 1116

We're not blocking housing statewide — only exempting our disaster area so Altadena can recover before these density laws are expanded.
